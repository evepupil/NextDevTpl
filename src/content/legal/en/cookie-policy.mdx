# Cookie Policy

Last updated: February 6, 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how NextjsTpl ("we", "us", or "our") uses cookies and similar technologies when you visit our website or use our Service. It describes what these technologies are, why we use them, and your choices.

## 1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. Cookies can be "session" cookies (deleted when you close your browser) or "persistent" cookies (remain until they expire or you delete them).

## 2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

### 2.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the Service to function, such as authentication and security.

**Examples (may vary):** session identifiers, CSRF tokens, language preference.

### 2.2 Functional Cookies

These cookies remember preferences and settings to improve your experience.

**Examples (may vary):** theme selection, cookie consent, UI preferences.

### 2.3 Analytics Cookies

If enabled, analytics cookies help us understand usage and improve the Service.

**Examples (may vary):** Vercel Analytics or similar tools.

### 2.4 Marketing Cookies

We do not use marketing cookies by default. If we introduce them, we will ask for your consent and update this policy.

## 3. Third-Party Cookies

Third-party providers may set cookies when you use their services through NextjsTpl, including:

- **Creem** (payments)
- **Google / GitHub** (OAuth authentication)
- **Analytics or monitoring providers** (if enabled)

## 4. Your Choices

### 4.1 Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit our site, you can accept all cookies, accept only necessary cookies, or manage preferences.

### 4.2 Browser Controls

Most browsers let you view, delete, or block cookies. If you disable cookies, some features may not work properly.

## 5. Similar Technologies

We may also use local storage or session storage for preferences and temporary data.

## 6. Updates to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. We will post the updated policy and revise the "Last updated" date.
